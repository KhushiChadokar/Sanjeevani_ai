Frequently Asked Questions

Thalassemia is an inherited blood disorder that affects the body's ability to produce hemoglobin and red blood cells. People with thalassemia have fewer healthy red blood cells and less hemoglobin than normal.
The frequency depends on the severity of your condition. Most patients with thalassemia major need transfusions every 2-4 weeks, while those with thalassemia intermedia may need them less frequently.
Signs include fatigue, joint pain, abdominal pain, irregular heartbeat, and changes in skin color. Regular monitoring and iron chelation therapy help manage iron levels.
With proper treatment and care, many people with thalassemia can live full, productive lives. Regular medical care, blood transfusions, and iron chelation therapy are key to managing the condition.
Focus on a balanced diet rich in calcium, vitamin D, and folic acid. Limit iron-rich foods and avoid vitamin C supplements with meals, as they increase iron absorption.
